SEIZE
Mobile Application January 2015
SEIZE is a mobile application that helps users understand their distraction better through self-tracking. There are couple applications that help people stay away from those distractors by blocking certain websites, however, those are mostly temporary treatments and mainly concentrated on negative reinforcement.

SEIZE takes a different stand : it helps users self-track their main distractors with notifications
and provides a real-time data visualization of the amount of time that has been wasted on distractors.
SEIZE connects the wasted time to an alternative future-- activities that could have been made possible with the time spent on distractors -- thus, allow users to stay motivated in changing their behavior patterns.

What? It is already midnight?
January 2015
Professor Fabio Franchino gave us a brief to outline a new service/mobile app that allow self-knowledge throught self-tracking.
While other teams focused on tracking some tangible things, such as amount of water or food consumed, we wanted to experiment a bit.
Ideas went wild : we talked about counting the number of times people smile per day, number of times one think about the specific person and etc.

The final idea that we agreed on was about 'lost time.'
Let's track lost time.Let users choose a specific website/social network and track how much time is wasted on those distractors.
Considering that we are always living with distractions, it might be interesting to visualize how much time is actually spent on those.
Positive attitude
January 2015
Since the ultimate goal of the app was to let users be aware of the lost time and change their behaviors we naturally sought how to design an effective behavior intervention. Of course, we could simpy tell 'You, reatrd. You spent 24 hours (a day!) on FB this week."

However, we wanted to bring some positive attitude.
We decided to provide an alternative future. "With the amount of time you lost, you could do .... this!" Of course, 'this' can vary based on the user's setting.
Data Input & Visualization at the same time
January 2015
We spent lots of time thinking about how to track this lost time.
Technically, tracking usage information through data log was impossible.
Of course, we can ask users to report their distraction at the end of the day,
but then the report wouldn't be accurate and it is verly likely to forget the reporting.

We settled on giving a notification in a predefined interval, and user can simply report if they are using any distracting services. Through this yes/no answer, the app can get how fequently one is being distrated.

I wanted to add another kind of behavior intervention through the self-report, which led to this blocking interface. When the user taps on the distractor, the black bar of the selected distractor gets longer blocking the alterantive future.
Personal Tracking Report
January 2015
The tracking result interface is designed to emphasize the lost time and what user could do with that time. The personal tone is being maintained with corresponding data visualization.

Interaction flow
YOUSHARE October 2014
After the 3 days of user research and feature analysis, we envisioned a mobile application that takes a whole different approach on house sesarch. YouShare changes the paradigm of current house search from property-person match to inhabitants match. youShare matches and connects future housemates based on their needs and preferences. Housemates who are searching for a newcomer and the newcomer will be connected, and naturally property information will be delivered in a personal and reliable manner.

A common behavior pattern found in the user resesarch was, people usually start house searching (i.e, going through postings on the website) more than three months in advance but they do not get serious about it (making phone calls) until the moving date comes close. Therefore, the key feature of an app is an automated search (based upon the profile) and real-time notification.
We follow the flow of the dating apps; user will first make a profile verified by an email address or phone number, and answer various questions that are closely related to life as a housemate. This profile making process becomes a preliminary verification system. Then the user will see the result of matches. This match result can be filtered, for example, one can see the housemate who already has secured a place. We went through couple rounds of wireframing with an advice session from Frog design to come up with.

R-You
Interactive Object October 2014
1 out of 68 children are born with Autism Spectrum Disorders.
Children with Autism have difficulties in various domains but most conspicuously,
communication and social abilities. Around 40 to 60 thousand dollars per year are spent for the behavior therapy for a single child with autism but there is no perfect cure identified yet.

So, various methods are used to help children with autism. Games have been recognized as a very efficient training approach for it holds the children's attention most effectively. However, so far in the market, there is only very few games designed specifically for social learning of children with autism spectrum disordres. Those games were mostly based on dolls, cards, or screens, with no real interactions with another person.

R-You is the only game with a tangible interface that facilitates social interaction
for children with autism spectrum disorder, in particular for children with high functioning autism.

R-you October 2014
The principle of R-you is like this.
Two players sit in front of each other with R-you in between
They look into their eyes through an eye hole of R-you.
A quizzer says something and a guess determines if that statement was from the R-you's message box or from the quizzer.

The message box includes statements like
"You always look nice, but unusually nice today."
"I think you are secretly into me."
"This game is really cool."
"I think you must be a fantastic dancer."

R-you October 2014
We only had 3 days to get this project done.
Therefore, Francesca and I decided to divide the work and focus on our specialties.
She worked on digital fabrication and I worked on arduino circuits and code. We initially wanted to have a digital LED screen that shows various messages, however, the LED screen didn't get connected well. The message box has to rotate 180 degree in each game, but this rotation made the screen connection unstable. So we decided to use an analogue message box, with rolled statments inside.

RIVOLARE
Advanced Service March 2015
Flying the sky is one of the most consistently pursued dreams of human beings.
Nowadays, lots of people are enjoying paraglidings, however,
there is no good service that helps people share the experience of flying sky.

Rivolare is an integrated service targeted for paragliders. It provides an opportunity to discover new paragliding experiences, capture and relive their own, and share them even during the ride through live broadcast. Rivolare empowers the paragliding user by giving them a tool to encapsulate the meaningful moments as well as sharing those to people and community.

Advanced Service
RIVOLARE March 2015
The brief given by Frog Design was to design an advaced service with the focus on sports.
There were several criteria that makes 'advanced' service : it has to be
connected (a service can be everywhere, anytime, always ready and available for the customer, regardless of channels, physical locations and time of the day),
smart(be responsitve to various situations and learn from feedback data)
and user-centered.

While the description was rather grand, in the end, it was about
designing a service that can connect various dots : the need of user, the various technologies and devices, and data mining technology.
Initial task for us was to understand the user, paragliders, since none of the team members was a professional flier.
User Research
RIVOLARE March 2015
For it was not easy to have a in-person interviews,
we focused on getting contacts through paragliding communities in the web.
We joined more than 80 facebook groups and posted a notification about brief introduction of our project and a contact information along with a survey link.

Based on 7 in person interviews (either in real life or via skype) and 32 survey ansewrs, we created 4 user archetypes.
Exsiting paragliding related services were also analyzed based on the feature. Then, the services were categorized by the user experience that has been discovered through user research.
